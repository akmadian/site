Homelab

What is a Homelab?

A homelab is a sandbox in which one can learn and play with new or unfamiliar technologies. They can be as simple as a set of VMs on an old PC or laptop, or as complex as multiple enterprise grade servers working in tandem.

Why do I have a homelab?

I originally started putting all of this together when I decided to move away from using larger tech corporations and their practices of data mining, along with some other ethical conflicts I find with them. I moved all of my files from Google Drive to Open Media Vault and NextCloud, Google Calendar to BaiKal, Gmail to Proton Mail, etc.

The only thing I really have yet to shake is YouTube, I just find the variety and quality of content to be so much better than competing platforms like BitChute or Odysee.

It's a fantastic way for me to learn more about networking (something I've wanted to do for a long time), and to scratch my hardware tinkering itch.

Also, just because it's a lot of fun :)

My Homelab

What do I use it for?

I have most everything running in Debian VMs in ProxMox hosts. I enjoy the stability and quality of documentation for Debian. I've got two main hosts that I have running constantly:

  • An interesting little SOC board I found on Ebay, a Norco MITX-6948, running ProxMox.
    • PiHole for ad and tracker blocking
    • Wireguard so I can access files and PiHole on my phone and laptop when away from home
    • OPNsense for routing
    • 2 Nginx Proxy Manager instances, one for LAN DNS, and one for publicly accessible services
    • Portainer
    • Grafana when I want some neat stats
    • BaiKal for calendars and contacts
  • A heavily modded 3u SuperMicro storage server, also running ProxMox.
    • Open Media Vault for a NAS OS
    • NextCloud
    • Jellyfin for a media server
